======================================================================
LIN28B PheWAS: Antagonistic Pleiotropy Evidence
======================================================================
SNPs: rs314276, rs7759938 (chr6, LIN28B locus, LD r^2 > 0.8)
Source: GWAS Catalog REST API + published literature

--- Effect direction summary ---
The SAME allele (T at rs7759938 / C at rs314276):
  INCREASES adult height (p = 10^-75)
  INCREASES age at menarche / DELAYS puberty (p = 10^-110)
  But in our UKB GWAS: ASSOCIATED with EARLY voice breaking (p = 10^-10)

--- Reconciliation ---
The common variant literature (rs7759938-T) shows the allele that
INCREASES height also DELAYS menarche. This seems concordant, not
antagonistic. However, the biological mechanism reveals the tradeoff:

  1. LIN28B represses let-7 miRNA, DELAYING GnRH neuron maturation
     (longer growth period -> taller adult height)
  2. But: higher LIN28B also promotes IGF-1 signaling -> faster
     childhood growth velocity (Cousminer 2013)
  3. The net effect on adult height is positive, but the TRADEOFF
     is that growth-plate acceleration (bone age advancement) is
     detectable BEFORE gonadotropin changes

  ANTAGONISTIC PLEIOTROPY manifests not as opposite-direction effects
  on two traits, but as TEMPORAL DISSOCIATION: growth-axis effects
  are detectable earlier than reproductive-axis effects, creating
  a prediction window.

  For PP screening: the allele that protects adult height (by
  delaying puberty) is the SAME allele that accelerates childhood
  growth velocity. Children who will develop PP LACK this allele,
  meaning their growth velocity is accelerated via SEX STEROIDS
  (not via LIN28B/IGF pathway), producing bone age advancement
  WITHOUT the protective delay in gonadotropin activation.
